## 1. Temporal graphs & how they are learned

### 1.1 Representations
Dynamic graphs come in two forms (Kazemi et al., *Representation Learning for
Dynamic Graphs: A Survey*, JMLR 2020): **discrete-time dynamic graphs (DTDG)** =
an ordered sequence of static snapshots; **continuous-time dynamic graphs (CTDG)**
= an initial state plus a stream of timestamped events (edge add/delete, feature
updates). CTDG is the harder, richer target: it preserves exact ordering and
inter-event times, needs no window size, and demands streaming consumption.
**Attributed** temporal graphs carry time-varying features; **heterogeneous /
temporal-knowledge** graphs add typed nodes and relations (quadruples *(s,r,o,t)*).

**→ For AGORA:** we emit CTDG event streams (the most expressive surface), typed
and attribute-bearing — strictly more than snapshot generators.

### 1.2 Temporal GNNs (the models that consume our data)
- **JODIE** — coupled RNNs + an embedding-projection operator that ages embeddings between interactions (Kumar, Zhang & Leskovec, KDD 2019).
- **DyRep** — a temporal point process with two-time-scale deep recurrent intensities (Trivedi et al., ICLR 2019).
- **TGAT** — self-attention over temporal neighborhoods with a functional **time encoding** (Xu et al., ICLR 2020).
- **TGN** — per-node **memory** + embedding module; subsumes JODIE/TGAT/DyRep (Rossi et al., 2020; `VERIFY` workshop vs. conf.).
- **CAW** — causal anonymous walks encode motifs inductively by relative position (Wang et al., ICLR 2021).
- **GraphMixer** — fixed time-encoding + MLP-Mixer; shows no RNN/attention needed (Cong et al., ICLR 2023).
- **DyGFormer** — Transformer over 1-hop interaction sequences w/ neighbor co-occurrence encoding (Yu et al., NeurIPS 2023; *DyGLib*).
- **EdgeBank** — non-learning memorization baseline (Poursafaei et al., NeurIPS 2022).

### 1.3 Tasks
Future/temporal **link prediction** (transductive + inductive); dynamic **node
classification** (time-varying labels); **anomaly / event detection** (the task a
controllable-ground-truth generator most directly serves).

**→ For AGORA:** these are our customers. The anomaly-detection task is exactly the
one current data underserves — our labels unlock it.

## 2. Anomaly: definitions & detection

### 2.1 Definitions
Hawkins (1980): an observation "generated by a different mechanism." Three
conflated senses — ontological (different mechanism), normative (rule violation),
epistemic (model surprise); only the epistemic one is touched by "knowing"
(see `mustread.txt` §3, the *knowing ≠ controlling* argument).

### 2.2 Taxonomy
Generic: **point / contextual / collective** anomalies (Chandola, Banerjee & Kumar,
*ACM Comp. Surveys*, 2009). Graph/dynamic: **node / edge / subgraph / event(change-
point)** levels (Ranshous et al., *WIREs Comp. Stat.* 2015; Ma et al., *IEEE TKDE*
2021; Ekle & Eberle, *TKDD* 2024).

### 2.3 Temporal-graph anomaly methods
NetWalk (Yu et al., KDD 2018), AddGraph (Zheng et al., IJCAI 2019), StrGNN (Cai et
al., CIKM 2021), TADDY (Liu et al., *TKDE* 2021/22 `VERIFY`), SAD (Tian et al.,
IJCAI 2023 `VERIFY`), SLADE (Lee et al., KDD 2024 `VERIFY`).

### 2.4 Camouflage / adversarial realism
Real fraud minimizes separability: **CARE-GNN** distinguishes **feature
camouflage** and **relation camouflage** (connecting to benign nodes to dilute the
neighborhood) (Dou et al., CIKM 2020).

### 2.5 Evaluation pitfalls
**Negative sampling**: one random negative per positive inflates scores; **TGB**
fixed this with multiple + historical (hard) negatives and MRR (Huang et al.,
NeurIPS 2023 D&B `VERIFY` year; foundational fragility shown by Poursafaei et al.,
NeurIPS 2022). Under extreme imbalance, **AUC-ROC is over-optimistic**; **AUC-PR /
Average Precision** is more discriminative (Davis & Goadrich, ICML 2006);
**precision@k** matches a fixed alert budget.

**→ For AGORA:** our 5 control axes (prevalence, separability/difficulty, type-mix,
placement, cascade) + label=intent let us produce *controllable difficulty* and
report AUC-PR / precision@k unambiguously, and directly stress-test the negative-
sampling regime.

## 3. Graph generation models (the lineage; mechanisms)

### 3.1 Classic random-graph models
- **Erdős–Rényi** G(n,p): independent edges; phase transitions + small diameter; misses heavy tails/clustering/community.
- **Barabási–Albert**: degree-proportional attachment → power law (fixed α≈3); misses tunable α, clustering, assortativity.
- **Watts–Strogatz**: rewired ring → high clustering + short paths (small-world); degree homogeneous.
- **Price / copying** (Price 1965/76; Kumar et al., FOCS 2000): cumulative advantage / link copying → power laws + bipartite communities.
- **Duplication–divergence** (Vázquez 2003; Chung–Lu 2003): duplicate+perturb → tunable power law + modularity (biology).
- **Configuration model** (Bollobás 1980; Molloy–Reed 1995; Newman–Strogatz–Watts 2001): exact degree sequence, ~zero clustering, no community.
- **R-MAT** (Chakrabarti, Zhan & Faloutsos, SDM 2004): recursive adjacency-matrix quadrants; heavy tails, fast (Graph500); degree oscillations, weak clustering.
- **Stochastic Kronecker** (Leskovec et al., JMLR 2010; KRONFIT): Kronecker powers of an initiator; heavy tails + densification; multinomial degree artifacts.
- **Forest Fire** (Leskovec, Kleinberg & Faloutsos, KDD 2005/TKDD 2007): recursive "burning" → densification, shrinking diameters, communities.
- **SBM** (Holland et al. 1983; degree-corrected Karrer–Newman 2011) & **LFR** (Lancichinetti et al., PRE 2008): explicit community structure (LFR = the community-detection standard, tunable μ).
- **Random geometric graphs** (Gilbert 1961; Penrose 2003): proximity in metric space → spatial locality, high clustering, large diameter, no hubs.
- **BTER** (Seshadhri, Kolda & Pinar, PRE 2012; SISC 2014 `VERIFY` 2012 vs 2011): ER blocks by degree + Chung–Lu layer → joint degree + degree-dependent clustering at scale.

### 3.2 Scalable generators
- **TrillionG** (Park & Kim, SIGMOD 2017): a **Recursive Vector** model unifying R-MAT (whole-edge scope) and Kronecker (an-edge scope) via a per-vertex scope; O(d_max) space, trillion edges on 10 PCs in <2 h.
- **S3G2 / LDBC SNB Datagen** (Pham, Boncz & Erling, TPCTC 2012): property dictionaries + edges along **correlation dimensions** via MapReduce sliding windows → correlated, realistic, scalable social graphs.
- **gMark** (Bagan et al., *IEEE TKDE* 2017): schema-driven, generates graph **+ matched query workload** with selectivity control (controllability over scale).

### 3.3 Deep generative graph models (and why they don't scale)
GraphRNN (You et al., ICML 2018, ~2K nodes), GraphVAE (Simonovsky & Komodakis,
ICANN 2018, tens of nodes), NetGAN (Bojchevski et al., ICML 2018, ~3K), GRAN (Liao
et al., NeurIPS 2019, ~5K), graph **diffusion** (DiGress, ICLR 2023; GDSS, ICML
2022) — tens–hundreds of nodes. Nearly all materialize an O(n²) adjacency → far
below classic generators' 10⁶–10⁹.

**→ For AGORA:** our pluggable topology layer (`mustread.txt` §8) offers these
classic models as the "skeleton"; we get classic scale + simulation semantics.
Deep generators are out of the hot path (they can't scale); we may use them only
offline for niche structure priors if ever.

## 4. Graph properties & fidelity metrics

### 4.1 Properties
Power law P(k)∝k^(−α), 2<α<3 — **fit rigorously** with MLE + KS-minimized x_min +
bootstrap p-value (Clauset, Shalizi & Newman, *SIAM Review* 2009; many "power laws"
fail, often log-normal — Broido & Clauset, *Nat. Commun.* 2019). Clustering
coefficient (local avg vs global transitivity differ); **modularity** (Newman–
Girvan, PRE 2004); **effective diameter** = 90th-pct shortest path (Palmer et al.,
KDD 2002); **assortativity** (Newman, PRL 2002); **densification power law** +
**shrinking diameters** (Leskovec et al., KDD 2005).

### 4.2 Synthetic-vs-real fidelity
**MMD** (Gretton et al., JMLR 2012) = RKHS distance between distributions;
**GraphRNN** evaluates MMD over graph-statistic distributions (degree, clustering,
orbit counts) with a Wasserstein/EMD kernel; **GRAN** adds Laplacian-spectrum MMD.
**KS distance** for scalar CDFs (tail-insensitive, univariate). **Caveats**: MMD is
sensitive to kernel/σ/binning, has no inherent scale, can be non-monotonic (O'Bray
et al., ICLR 2022); spectral comparison is O(n³).

**→ For AGORA:** these are precisely what `agora-introspect` computes (streaming) and
what the validation phase reports vs real datasets — the direct answer to demo
reviewer critique C5/W1 and Risk R3 ("measured, not asserted, realism").

## 5. Simulation foundations

### 5.1 Agent-based modeling (ABM)
Autonomous interacting agents whose local rules produce emergent macro-behavior
(Bonabeau, *PNAS* 2002; Macal & North, *J. Simulation* 2010; Epstein & Axtell,
*Growing Artificial Societies*, 1996). ABM + DES compose: agent decisions = events.

### 5.2 Discrete-event simulation (DES)
**Future-event list** (priority queue), a **simulation clock** that jumps to the
next event, and an **event loop** that pops the min-timestamp event, advances the
clock, runs its handler (which may schedule new events). O(log n)/event with a heap
(Banks et al.; Law & Kelton — standard texts).

### 5.3 Time-stepped vs event-driven
Time-stepped advances by fixed Δt (simple, vectorizable, wastes empty steps, can
mis-order); event-driven jumps to the next event (no idle steps, exact order, heap
overhead). Cost scales with #steps vs #events respectively.

### 5.4 Parallel DES (PDES)
Partition into logical processes exchanging timestamped messages (Fujimoto, *CACM*
1990); each LP must obey the **local causality constraint**. **Conservative**
(Chandy–Misra, *IEEE TSE* 1979; Bryant MIT thesis 1977): process only when no
earlier event can arrive; **null messages** + **lookahead**. **Optimistic** (Time
Warp; Jefferson, *ACM TOPLAS* 1985): speculate, **rollback** on stragglers,
**antimessages**, **Global Virtual Time** bounds rollback/memory.

### 5.5 Reproducible parallel RNG
**Counter-based** (Philox/Threefry; Salmon et al., SC 2011 — stateless, seekable,
pass BigCrush); **PCG** (O'Neill, tech report 2014 `VERIFY` — never peer-reviewed);
**SplitMix** (Steele, Lea & Flood, OOPSLA 2014 — splittable streams, Java's
`SplittableRandom`).

### 5.6 Fast sampling
**Alias method** O(1)/sample, O(n) table (Walker, *TOMS* 1977; Vose, *IEEE TSE*
1991 stable construction); **inverse-CDF**; **rejection** (Devroye 1986).

**→ For AGORA:** event-driven **DES** is our engine paradigm (cost ∝ #events);
**PDES** is how we scale across cores/nodes (causal time-block partitioning);
**counter-based RNG** gives bit-deterministic parallel reproducibility (a hard
requirement, `mustread.txt` §7); **alias sampling** is the O(1) counterparty-
selection primitive that keeps the hot path fast (Risk R1).

## 6. Streaming & sketch algorithms (single-pass introspection)

- **HyperLogLog** — cardinality; error ≈1.04/√m, O(log log n)-bit registers (Flajolet et al., AofA 2007).
- **Count-Min Sketch** — frequencies; O((1/ε)log(1/δ)) space, ε·‖a‖₁ error (Cormode & Muthukrishnan, 2005).
- **KLL** — ε-quantiles; O((1/ε)log log(1/δ)) space, provably optimal (Karnin, Lang & Liberty, FOCS 2016).
- **t-digest** — quantiles with high tail accuracy, mergeable (Dunning & Ertl, arXiv 2019 `VERIFY` vs 2021 paper).
- **Greenwald–Khanna** — deterministic ε-quantiles, O((1/ε)log(εN)) (SIGMOD 2001).
- **Reservoir sampling** — uniform k-sample in one pass (Vitter, *TOMS* 1985).
- **Streaming triangle counting / degree estimation** — DOULION sparsification (Tsourakakis et al., KDD 2009); survey: McGregor, *SIGMOD Record* 2014.

**→ For AGORA:** these power single-pass output introspection (`mustread.txt` §11b)
so 1B+-edge datasets stay measurable without a second pass — the systems core of
the "introspection co-designed with scale" leg.

## 7. RAG systems (our self-built rule-authoring pipeline)

- **RAG** — couple a generator with a retriever, conditioning on retrieved passages (Lewis et al., NeurIPS 2020).
- **Embeddings** — Sentence-BERT (Reimers & Gurevych, EMNLP 2019); **BGE** (Xiao et al., 2023 `VERIFY` SIGIR 2024 retitle); **E5** (Wang et al., 2022).
- **ANN search** — **FAISS** IVF/PQ/GPU (Johnson, Douze & Jégou, *IEEE TBD* 2019/21 `VERIFY`); **HNSW** multi-layer proximity graphs (Malkov & Yashunin, *TPAMI* 2020 `VERIFY`; arXiv 2016).
- **Hybrid retrieval** — BM25 (Robertson & Zaragoza, *FnTIR* 2009) + dense.
- **Chunking** — engineering practice, no canonical paper (`VERIFY`: cite library docs).
- **Constrained decoding** — guaranteed-valid output via FSM-indexed vocabulary; regex/JSON-schema (**outlines**, Willard & Louf, arXiv 2023); **GBNF** grammars (llama.cpp).
- **Small open LLMs + serving** — Qwen2.5 (2024), Phi-3.5 (2024), Llama-3 (2024); **vLLM PagedAttention** (Kwon et al., SOSP 2023); **Ollama** (repo).

**→ For AGORA:** this is the §9 OFFLINE rule-authoring pipeline. **Constrained
decoding** guarantees valid typed configs (no hallucinated schema); **FAISS +
sentence-transformers + vLLM are already in the the cluster conda env**. The LLM never
touches the per-event hot path.

## 8. Benchmarks & what makes a good one

- **LDBC SNB** — **choke-point-driven design**: workloads engineered to hit specific technical difficulties (Erling et al., SIGMOD 2015); the model for an industrially-credible benchmark.
- **TGB / TGB 2.0** — curated real temporal datasets + rigorous evaluation (NOT a generator) (Huang et al., NeurIPS 2023; Gastinger et al., 2024).
- **GADBench** — supervised graph-anomaly-detection benchmark on static/small graphs (Tang et al., NeurIPS 2023); **DGraph** — real labeled financial graph (Huang et al., NeurIPS 2022).
- **Reproducibility** — SIGMOD/PVLDB require code+data+scripts; benchmark/dataset papers must ship a full reproducibility package and release publicly.

**→ For AGORA:** we target SIGMOD **[Experiments & Analysis]**; the bar is
**fidelity + reproducibility + community value**. We **complement TGB**
(generation vs curation) and reach the **billion-edge regime GADBench/DGraph
cannot** — and supply controllable ground truth they cannot.
